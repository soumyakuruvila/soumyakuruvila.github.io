    Projects and Research

    Mechatronics Research - The FlexShoe

    The FlexShoe is a product for those who are suffering from foot swelling. This product relieves the pain by using a mechanism constructed within the shoe, that is able to spread the area in the front of the shoe so that there is more space for the swollen foot. Many people suffer from swollen feet and face difficulties with outdoor activities primarily because their shoe is tight and uncomfortable. As a solution to this problem, we have come up with the idea to make the FlexShoe which offers the option to adjust the width of the shoe to fit a swollen foot. This helps because an average shoe causes pain due to the enormous amount of pressure applied to the swollen parts of your foot by your shoe. Inside our revolutionized shoe, arduino motors and gears are installed and are able to adjust the width of the shoe to provide space and increase comfortability for your foot. The inside of the shoe will be modified with memory foam to offer extra comfort. This way the shoe will conform to the shape of your foot and it won’t be as uncomfortable. As of 2012, 29.1 percent of Americans suffer from diabetes. Also, other diseases that cause foot swelling include Lymphedema, Venous Insufficiency and ankle/foot injuries. In addition, pregnancies are one of the main causes for swelling of the foot as well. The FlexShoe will be able to target a large percentage of customers who suffer from foot swelling and will be available in an affordable price range as well as many different sizes. The FlexShoe is a revolutionary innovation and will help millions of people worldwide. No longer will it be a pain to walk in your shoe.

    Nanotechnology Research - Lithium Ion Battery

    The name of my research project is the The Use of Fluoride treated Silicon Nanoparticles for a better Lithium Ion Batttery. I conducted this research by myself under the mentorship of Dr. Kim. I began this research during my sophomore year of high school and have since one a medal from the Office of Naval Research at the New Jersey Regional Science Fair. This research was conducted in order to develop a high quality Lithium Ion battery through the use of Fluoride Treated Silicon Pedot PSS. Solutions of Silicon Pedot PSS were synthesized through repetitive processes of filtering and centrifuging. HF was later added to increase conductivity of the solution by reacting with the Silicon Dioxide that coated and protected the silicon nanoparticles. The solutions will be coated on to anodes and measured for power performance which was higher than a normal lithium ion battery. By adding the silicon to the solution, the overall conductivity is increased, thereby increasing the conductivity of the battery. In the end, this research will provide a better, more-powerful compact battery.

    FIRST Robotics - Team 6203

    FIRST Robotics or For Inspiration and Recognition of Science and Technology is a robotics competetion recognized globally. It includes several competitions including the FIRST Lego League, the FIRST Tech Challenge and the FIRST Robotics Competition. There are more than 6000 teams worldwide and this past year our school started a FIRST Robotics team at our school. We are the Titanium Knights Team 6203, strong and proud. The FIRST Robotics Competition required an extremely rigorous amount of work and commitment from our entire team. Every year, a new challenge must be completed by a robot constructed in six weeks. Last year’s theme was FIRST Stronghold, where we had to build a robot capable of conquering several obstacles in order to capture and siege a tower. An extensive rulebook is published at the beginning of the season which specificies the do’s and dont’s of the competition. Creating a robot able to accomplish the task but also follow the rules is a tall task, but we were able to do it, successfully at that, as rookies. Rookies generally don’t accomplish as much as we did this past season. The year began with weeks of preparation leading up to the competition reveal signaling the beginning of the season. We had to secure sponsors, equipment, and mentors, all crucial to the team. When the competition was revealed, preparation began as we began to build a robot. We split into teams to maximize efficiency - Design, Sofware Build, Finance, Social Relations. There were many setbacks, trips to Home Depot and Lowe’s, and arguments galore. But, we all knew the final goal and all wanted to get there, together. After weeks, we finally had a design we could all agree on and feasibly build with the available equipment and budget. 6 weeks turned to 5 and 2. We began to see the outline of a robot and were elated at the process. But, there was a complication. After looking over the design and math, some of us saw a possible problem with the gear box. There was a slight miscalculation, but we couldn’t be sure whether it would affect the outcome. We had enough members working on the primary, that we were able to sacrifice a team of little less than 10 to work on a backup bot. It was a simple design and would be primarily geared for defense. 2 became 1 and 1 became a little less than 2 days. I remember the day. We had just finished the primary bot and set it on its first test run. It failed. We checked and checked, but we couldn't fix the problem in less than 24 hours. We voted and knew what the sensbible thing to do was. we took the backup bot to the competion, all low on fumes. We didnrsquo;t expect much from the The Millenium Defalcon (Named after our teacher), but we nonetheless gave it all we got. Defense was our identity, and we were darn good at it. We became recognized as a Defensive Specialist, leading with our teams to two District Event Championships and eventually the MAR District Championships, not to mention a Rookie All-star award. This was extremely rare for a rookie. We all reflected on the season with high hopes for the next. We knew we weren’t done.
